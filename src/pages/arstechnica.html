Week in gaming: Duke Forever, Battlefield 3, Skyrim

By Ben Kuchera | Published June 18, 2011 2:00 PMLast updated June 18, 2011 2:43 PM

So... played any good games lately? Our admittedly harsh review of Duke Nukem Forever drew strong thoughts from both sides of the debate, as did the resulting PR implosion when a company working on the game's publicity threatened to blacklist certain outlets based on poor reviews. If I thought I had it bad having to play this game and then continually talk about it, I have to admit that's nothing next to losing your job for trying to defend it.

In better news, Skyrim looks great, and we know a bit more about the power of the Wii U. It has been quite the week, so stop by and see what stories you might have missed.

Duke Nukem Forever: barely playable, not funny, rampantly offensive: Duke Nukem Forever is finally here—and it's one of the worst games from a major studio in quite some time. The jokes border on hateful. The graphics are a blurry mess. The shooting is unsatisfying. The good news? It's short.

Duke Nukem's PR threatens to punish sites that run negative reviews: An explicit threat was made by a PR company working with 2K Games: since some of the Duke Nukem Forever reviews went "too far," some outlets could be frozen out from future review copies of games. An apology came later, but for the first time a PR firm went public with the possibility of a blacklist.

Battlefield 3 preorder weapon and item bonuses annoy fans: If you preorder Battlefield 3 at one of two retailers in the UK, you'll receive a free content pack that gives you powerful weapons and items on the first day of release. Fans of the game, unsurprisingly, have an issue with this.

LulzSec hackers demand hats, threaten release of Brink user data: The hacker group LulzSec has been publicizing an attack on Bethesda Software, complete with threats to release the personal information of those who play Brink. The group's demands? MOAR SKYRIM.

Report: Wii U 50 percent more powerful than PS3 (and it should be): An analyst claims cites unnamed developers to the effect that the Wii U is 50 percent more powerful than the Playstation 3—to which Ars responds, "Boy, we sure hope so."

A reason to hate Pixar: Uniracers on the SNES is a masterpiece: Uniracers was one of the best games to grace the Super Nintendo, but few managed to play it and fewer seem to remember. Ars explores a forgotten classic, and explains why it's hard to find.

Skyrim's dragon battles top off impressive demo: It's hard to judge a game like Elder Scrolls 5: Skyrim by watching someone else play it, but the details and combat shown in our demo were enough to make us want the game in a way that's animalistic.

Torchlight 2: LAN play confirmed, new class announced, mods incoming: At E3 we sat down to play Torchlight 2 with Runic Games CEO Max Schaefer to talk about co-op play, mods, and why they're sticking with the PC as the game's lead platform. Runic's philosophy? Keep the company small, tackle one thing at a time, and keep the quality high.

Mass Effect 3, Forza 4 Kinect support are evidence of shifting strategy: Microsoft launched the Kinect with a variety of games that used only motion controls, but at E3 the company showed off a number of titles that use the Kinect hardware to enhance gameplay instead of driving it. This is a step in the right direction.

Nothing too crazy: why Adult Swim may be the best indie game publisher: By courting talented indie devs and paying them to create crazy games, Adult Swim has quietly become quite possibly the best indie publisher online.
